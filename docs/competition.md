# Competition Analysis

## Goal

Understand the current market.

Identify where RepeatShot can be meaningfully different.

The goal is NOT to invent a new category.

The goal is to build the easiest, most enjoyable app for documenting progress over time.

---

# Market Summary

The market already exists.

Several apps launched during 2025–2026 that solve portions of this problem.

Most competitors focus on one of four audiences:

- Before & After comparisons
- Content creators
- Professional documentation
- General repeat photography

No company appears to dominate the category.

This is encouraging.

It suggests there is demand but no clear winner.

---

# Competitor: ReShoot

Positioning

"Time machine for your camera."

Strengths

- Ghost overlay
- GPS awareness
- Automatic side-by-side
- Timeline
- Local storage
- Privacy-first
- No subscriptions

Weaknesses

- Heavy emphasis on location
- Less useful indoors
- Limited branding around projects
- Small user base

Lessons

People like simple.

Privacy matters.

Users don't want accounts.

"I wish the camera showed the previous image."

"I need project organization."

Business users repeatedly mention:

- detailing
- renovations
- rentals
- contractors

Consumers mention:

- babies
- fitness
- gardens
- vacations

# Opportunities

## Opportunity 1

Don't market a camera.

Market progress.

People care about the transformation.

Not the overlay.

---

## Opportunity 2

Projects first.

Every project tells a story.

Kitchen Remodel

Garden

Baby

Puppy

Vacation House

Fitness Journey

---

## Opportunity 3

Beautiful timeline.

The timeline should become the emotional center of the app.

Watching progress should feel rewarding.

---

## Opportunity 4

Reminders.

"Time to take your monthly garden photo."

This creates recurring engagement.

---

## Opportunity 5

Delight.

Tiny animations.

Clean UI.

Apple-quality interactions.

This category has surprisingly poor UX.

Manual camera controls.

Cloud collaboration.

---

# Our Position

RepeatShot isn't a better camera.

It's the easiest way to document change.

---

# Differentiation

Our promise is simple.

Open the project.

Line up the ghost image.

Capture.

---

# Risks

The category is becoming more crowded.

Ghost overlay alone is no longer unique.

Success will come from:

- Better onboarding
- Better branding
- Better design
- Better reminders
- Better organization
- Better emotional experience

Not from having more camera features.
